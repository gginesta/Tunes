Direction C — Festival

Bold, playful, unapologetically a party. Per-player colors, polaroid cards pinned to a corkboard, sticker halos, chunky 3D buttons, marker-hand annotations. Best for turning the game into a brand you'd put on a t-shirt.

Design notes

  • Per-player colors. Every player gets a signature color (rose · emerald · sky · amber · violet…) that follows them through score chips, avatars, challenge glows, and confetti — the app feels alive with your friends.
  • Polaroid timeline. Cards are pinned polaroids with a handwritten caption. The corkboard dot pattern, pins, and slight rotations give physical texture you can almost touch.
  • Multi-color wordmark. Each letter of TUNES sits in a different player color — it doubles as a legend for who's in the game.
  • 3D buttons. Buttons have a "press-down" shadow (no floaty flatness) making every action feel punchy on mobile.
  • Marker accents. Tiny hand-scrawled annotations ("hi there!", "jordan — place it!") humanize the UI — feels like playing with friends, not an interface.
